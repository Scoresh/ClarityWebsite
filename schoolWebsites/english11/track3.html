Analysis / Reasoning:

Lambada by Kaoma represents Holden Caulfield during his first day in New York City. First off, The song explores themes of nostalgia; the song often references how “the memory will always be there” or “the memory will be with him wherever he goes.” Holden reminisces on Jane Gallagher and how he almost made out with her. As the song dictates, Holden tries to remember this love that he had and he continuously attempts to kindle relationships with different people. He remembers Sally Hayes and he goes on a couple of dates with her, but it doesn’t end up working too well for him. He thinks about a “burlesque stripper” from Princeton, and tries to date her. “Well I’d like awfully to get together with you sometime, Mr. Cawffle. You sound very attractive.” (Salinger 73). While Holden isn’t crying in this point in time as the song would dictate, the song does predict that he would cry. In the last chapter, when Phoebe goes around the carousel, “(Holden) felt so damn happy all of a sudden, the way old Phoebe kept going around and around. I was damn near bawling, I felt so damn happy.” (Salinger 233). Holden experiences all of the emotions described in Lambada’s final lines: “Song, laughter and pain … a moment that hangs in the air.” (Kaoma 19-20).

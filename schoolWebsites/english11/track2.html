Analysis / Reasoning:

The second song in this tracklist involves Holden’s journey to New York City. Holden purchases a “One way ticket” to Penn Station. The lyrics of One Way Ticket describe a lonesome person leaving their “love” behind. The person stays at “Heartbreak Hotel” and cries a tear. The person in the song is also “never looking back.” Wow, do we see any similarities? Holden takes a train down to Penn Station. Before Holden goes on the train, however, he makes peace with Pencey Prep by yelling “sleep tight, morons!” Holden does say that he will miss his buddies back at Pencey, alluding to his “love” leaving him. Holden then stays at a cheap hotel, the Edmond hotel where he is appalled by the ideas of “sex.” Notably, he sees a couple squirting water into each other’s mouths, alluding to the following line: “Now lonely teardrops are all that I can see.” In such a time of loneliness, all Holden sees are “teardrops” (alluding to the water in the bathtub), and Holden continues to think about that throughout the chapter. Holden also cries physical teardrops: “All of a sudden I started to cry. I’d give anything if I hadn’t, but I did.” (Salinger 115). Finally, the “blues” mentioned in the song One Way Ticket represent Holden’s spiral to depression and cynical thoughts. At this point in the novel, Holden is heading towards a dark point in his life, and no one is there to stop him.

Analysis / Reasoning:

This final song, Don’t Dream it’s Over by Crowded House describes Holden’s behavior and feelings in the last couple of pages in the book. The first stanza of the song has the following lyrics: “There’s a battle ahead. Many battles are lost. But you’ll never see the end of the road.” (House 4-7). The collocation of these lyrics and Holden’s description of Phoebe on the carousel provide a clear outcome. Holden is doing his best to protect Phoebe, and although he made many mistakes (“Many battles are lost”), he extends his protection so Phoebe will never lose her innocence. The next notable lyrics are: “My possessions are causing me suspicion but there’s no proof. In the paper today, Tales of war and of waste.” (House 17-20) What does Holden hate the most? He hates the loss of innocence. So often, the news is filled with negativity and hate (or of war and waste in this case). Holden realizes this and he most likely would relate to these lyrics. The next lyrics, “Now I’m walking again. To the beat of a drum. … Get to know the feeling of liberation and release,” (House 29-35) describes Holden Caulfield perfectly. Holden is retracing his steps that he took the night before, this time sober, but he is free. Instead of being held back by alcohol or cigarettes, Holden is allowed to truly be himself and feels happy for the first time in a while. At this moment, Holden doesn’t want to dream that it’s over.

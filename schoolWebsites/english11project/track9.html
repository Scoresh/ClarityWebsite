Analysis / Reasoning:

Holden’s encounter with Mr. Antolini is a pivotal moment in The Catcher in the Rye. Holden finally loses the last person he truly trusts, and whether or not Mr. Antolini had bad intentions remains as an interpretation to the reader. However, the following lyrics relate to Holden Caulfield regardless of your interpretation of Mr. Antolini. The lyrics describe “Every breath you take, and every move you make. Every bond you break, every step you take, I’ll be watching you.” (Police 1-4) In The Catcher in the Rye, Holden sleeps at his old teacher's house. While Holden makes a few assumptions that Mr. Antolini is homosexual (referring that he never sees Mr. and Mrs. Antolini together in the same room), Holden still goes to bed because he's getting more and more sick. Then, the following scene happens: “I’ll be in the kitchen for a while – will the light bother you? ... I woke up all of a sudden. I don’t know what time it was or anything, but I woke up. I felt something on my head, some guy’s hand. Boy, it really scared hell out of me. What it was, it was Mr. Antolini’s hand.” (Salinger 211). Mr. Antolini is, literally, “watching” Holden. The last two lines of the chorus also juxtapose with the relationship between Caulfield and Antolini. “How my poor heart aches. With every step you take” (Police 11-12) Previously in the chapter, Mr. Antolini expresses concern for Caulfield’s character. “‘This fall I think you’re riding for—it’s a special kind of fall, a horrible kind.” (Salinger 207) Mr. Antolini’s “heart aches” because he doesn’t want to see Caulfield fall. With every “step” Holden takes, he spirals into a deeper and deeper depression. At this point in the novel, Holden has already spiraled to the point where it is noticeable by many characters, especially mr. Antolini.
